Rockfish

Rockfish typically weigh two to seven pounds and reach up to two feet in length. The flavor of rockfish is characterized by a lean, white, and flaky textured flesh as befits deep-dwelling cold water species. The heads and bones of rockfish make fantastic fish stock because they are very lean and clean tasting. Rockfish are also good raw in sushi, "cooked" by citrus in ceviche, or chopped in a tartare.

How To Store: Whole fish should be buried in ice with the belly cavity full of ice, since this is where deterioration happens most quickly. Adding ice to the belly slows this enzymatic process.

Range of Shelf Life: Three to seven days, depending on the quality of handling the fish has received.
